1. H'it's h'easy to be 'appy,
2. Don't you know;
3. There's no sense in being snappy,
4. Don't you know;
5. Wot's the use h'of being grumpy,
6. H'or a bally rotter mumpy?
7. Folks don't like a fellow dumpy,
8. Don't you know.
9. H'every one h'as fits h'of sadness,
10. Don't you know;
11. No ones life h'is wholly gladness,
12. Don't you know;
13. H'if todye you taste h'of sorrow,
14. H'or a shilling 'ave to borrow,
15. Frowning won't 'elp things tomorrow,
16. Don't you know.
17. O, a gloomy cus h'is silly,
18. Don't you know;
19. 'E's a downright balmy Billy,
20. Don't you know;
21. H'instead of h'alwyes moping
22. With 'is lips h'in downward sloping,
23. 'E should smile h'and keep on 'oping,
24. Don't you know.
25. O, my word! H'if I were willing,
26. Don't you know,
27. H'every time h'I'd lack a shilling,
28. Don't you know,
29. H'in me wye so very 'umble,
30. H'I could start a dismal mumble,
31. But h'I larf- -h'I never grumble- -
32. Don't you know.
33. Tyke the time the guv'or sacked me,
34. Don't you know;
35. good h'old chap!  'E'd alwyes backed me,
36. Don't you know;
37. 'E got 'ot and sent me flying,
38. Cut me h'off- -there's no use lying- -
39. But h'I larffed h'instead of crying,
40. Don't you know.
41. H'and h'I'm 'ere on h'earth, still grinning,
42. Don't you know;
43. Back once more h'at the beginning,
44. Don't you know;
45. Tyking life the wye h'I find it,
46. Never arskin' wot's be'ind it,
47. Though h'it's 'ard h'I do not mind it,
48. Don't you know.
