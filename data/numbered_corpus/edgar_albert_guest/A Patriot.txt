1. It's funny when a feller wants to do his little bit,
2. And wants to wear a uniform and lug a soldier's kit,
3. And ain't afraid of submarines nor mines that fill the sea,
4. They will not let him go along to fight for liberty
5. They make him stay at home and be his mother's darling pet,
6. But you can bet there'll come a time when they will want me yet.

7. I want to serve the Stars and Stripes, I want to go and fight,
8. I want to lick the Kaiser good, and do the job up right.
9. I know the way to use a gun and I can dig a trench
10. And I would like to go and help the English and the French.
11. But no, they say, you cannot march away to stirring drums;
12. Be mother's angel boy at home; stay there and twirl your thumbs.

13. I've read about the daring boys that fight up in the sky;
14. It seems to me that that must be a splendid way to die.
15. I'd like to drive an aeroplane and prove my courage grim
16. And get above a German there and drop a bomb on him,
17. But they won't let me go along to help the latest drive;
18. They say my mother needs me here because I'm only five
