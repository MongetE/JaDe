1. His mother's eyes are saddened, and her cheeks
2. are stained with tears,
3. And I'm facing now the struggle that I've
4. dreaded through the years;
5. For the boy that was our baby has been changed
6. into a man.
7. He's enlisted in the army as a true American.

8. He held her for a moment in his arms before
9. he spoke,
10. And I watched him as he kissed her, and it
11. seemed to me I'd choke,
12. For I knew just what was coming, and I knew
13. just what he'd done!
14. 'Another little mother had a soldier for a son.

15. When we'd pulled ourselves together, and the
16. first quick tears had dried,
17. We could see his eyes were blazing with the fire
18. of manly pride;
19. We could see his head was higher than it ever
20. was before,
21. For we had a man to cherish, and our baby was
22. no more.

23. Oh, I don't know how to say it! With the sorrow
24. comes the joy
25. That there isn't any coward in the make-up of
26. our boy.

28. with grief they're also hit,
