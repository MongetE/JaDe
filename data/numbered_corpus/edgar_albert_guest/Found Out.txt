1. "NEVER again,' said Mrs. Green, as she swayed in her rocking chair,
2. 'Never again will I think one house big enough for two to share;
3. Never again will I go away with another family,
4. I've had a month of that game this year, and once is enough for me.

5. 'I fried the fish and I stood all day in a kitchen stifling hot,
6. While Mrs. Burroughs, arrayed in pink, sat out in a shady spot;
7. And we were to share in the work, you know, but little the work she did;
8. Whenever there was any work to do she hurried away and hid.

9. 'Her children — impudent little brats — were always abusing mine,
10. They were always yelling for something, too!
11. In a week I was sick of their whine;
12. Oh, she's all very nice in town, I know, when her hair has the proper friz,
13. But you've only to live with that woman a week to know what she really is.'
