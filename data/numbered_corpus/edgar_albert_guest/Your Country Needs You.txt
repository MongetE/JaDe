1. The country needs a man like you,
2. It has a task for you to do.
3. It has a job for you to face.
4. Somewhere for you it has a place.
5. Not all the slackers dodge the work
6. Of service where the cannon lurk,
7. Not all the slackers on life's stage
8. Are boys of military age.
9. The old, the youthful and unfit
10. Must also do their little bit.

11. The country needs a man like you,'Twill suffer if you prove untrue.
12. What though you cannot bear a gun?
13. That isn't all that's to be done.
14. There are a thousand other ways
15. To serve your country through the days
16. Of trial and the nights of storm.
17. You need not wear a uniform
18. Or with the men in council sit
19. To serve the Flag and do your bit.

20. Somewhere for you there is a place,
21. Somewhere you have a task to face.
22. There's none so helpless or so frail
23. That cannot, when our foes assail,
24. In some way help our common cause
25. And be deserving of applause.
26. Behind the Flag we all must be,
27. Each at his post, awake to see
28. That in so far as he has striven,
29. His best was to his country given.

30. You can be patient, brave and strong,
31. And not complain when plans go wrong;
32. You can be cheerful at your toil,
33. Or till, perhaps, some patch of soil;
34. You can encourage others who
35. Have heavier, greater tasks to do;
36. You can be loyal, not in creed
37. Alone, but in each thought and deed;
38. You can make sacrifices, too.
