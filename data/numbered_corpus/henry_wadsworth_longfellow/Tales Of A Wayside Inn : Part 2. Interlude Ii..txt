1. 'I thought before your tale began,'
2. The Student murmured, 'we should have
3. Some legend written by Judah Rav
4. In his Gemara of Babylon;
5. Or something from the Gulistan,--
6. The tale of the Cazy of Hamadan,
7. Or of that King of Khorasan
8. Who saw in dreams the eyes of one
9. That had a hundred years been dead
10. Still moving restless in his head,
11. Undimmed, and gleaming with the lust
12. Of power, though all the rest was dust.

13. 'But lo! your glittering caravan
14. On the road that leadeth to Ispahan
15. Hath led us farther to the East
16. Into the regions of Cathay.
17. Spite of your Kalif and his gold,
18. Pleasant has been the tale you told,
19. And full of color; that at least
20. No one will question or gainsay.
21. And yet on such a dismal day
22. We need a merrier tale to clear
23. The dark and heavy atmosphere.
24. So listen, Lordlings, while I tell,
25. Without a preface, what befell
26. A simple cobbler, in the year ?
27. No matter; it was long ago;
28. And that is all we need to know.'
