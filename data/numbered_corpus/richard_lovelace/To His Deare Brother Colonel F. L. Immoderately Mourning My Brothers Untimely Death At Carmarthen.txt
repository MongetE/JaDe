1. I.
2. If teares could wash the ill away,
3. A pearle for each wet bead I'd pay;
4. But as dew'd corne the fuller growes,
5. So water'd eyes but swell our woes.

6. II.
7. One drop another cals, which still
8. (Griefe adding fuell) doth distill;
9. Too fruitfull of her selfe is anguish,
10. We need no cherishing to languish.

11. III.
12. Coward fate degen'rate man
13. Like little children uses, when
14. He whips us first, untill we weepe,
15. Then, 'cause we still a weeping keepe.

16. IV.
17. Then from thy firme selfe never swerve;
18. Teares fat the griefe that they should sterve;
19. Iron decrees of destinie
20. Are ner'e wipe't out with a wet eye.

21. V.
22. But this way you may gaine the field,
23. Oppose but sorrow, and 'twill yield;
24. One gallant thorough-made resolve
25. Doth starry influence dissolve.
