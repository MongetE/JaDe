1. DE SCAEVOLA.

2. Lictorem pro rege necans nunc mutius ultro
3. Sacrifico propriam concremat igne manum:
4. Miratur Porsenna virum, paenamque relaxans
5. Maxima cum obscessis faedera a victor init,
6. Plus flammis patriae confert quam fortibus armis,
7. Una domans bellum funere dextra sua.

8. ENGLISHED.

9. The hand, by which no king but serjeant dies,
10. Mutius in fire doth freely sacrifice;
11. The prince admires the Hero, quits his pains,
12. And Victor from the seige peace entertains;
13. Rome's more oblig'd to flames than arms or pow'r,
14. When one burnt hand shall the whole war devour.
