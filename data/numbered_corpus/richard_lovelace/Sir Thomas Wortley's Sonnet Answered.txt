1. [THE SONNET.

2. I.
3. No more
4. Thou little winged archer, now no more
5. As heretofore,
6. Thou maist pretend within my breast to bide,
7. No more,
8. Since cruell Death of dearest LYNDAMORE
9. Hath me depriv'd,
10. I bid adieu to love, and all the world beside.

11. II.
12. Go, go;
13. Lay by thy quiver and unbend thy bow
14. Poore sillie foe,
15. Thou spend'st thy shafts but at my breast in vain,
16. Since Death
17. My heart hath with a fatall icie deart
18. Already slain,
19. Thou canst not ever hope to warme her wound,
20. Or wound it o're againe.]


21. THE ANSWER.

22. I.
23. Againe,
24. Thou witty cruell wanton, now againe,
25. Through ev'ry veine,
26. Hurle all your lightning, and strike ev'ry dart,
27. Againe,
28. Before I feele this pleasing, pleasing paine.
29. I have no heart,
30. Nor can I live but sweetly murder'd with
31. So deare, so deare a smart.

32. II.
33. Then flye,
34. And kindle all your torches at her eye,
35. To make me dye
36. Her martyr, and put on my roabe of flame:
37. So I,
38. Advanced on my blazing wings on high,
39. In death became
40. Inthroan'd a starre, and ornament unto
41. Her glorious, glorious name.
