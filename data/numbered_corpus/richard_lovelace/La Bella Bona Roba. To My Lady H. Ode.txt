1. I.
2. Tell me, ye subtill judges in loves treasury,
3. Inform me, which hath most inricht mine eye,
4. This diamonds greatnes, or its clarity?

5. II.
6. Ye cloudy spark lights, whose vast multitude
7. Of fires are harder to be found then view'd,
8. Waite on this star in her first magnitude.

9. III.
10. Calmely or roughly!  Ah, she shines too much;
11. That now I lye (her influence is such),
12. Chrusht with too strong a hand, or soft a touch.

13. IV.
14. Lovers, beware! a certaine, double harme
15. Waits your proud hopes, her looks al-killing charm
16. Guarded by her as true victorious arme.

17. V.
18. Thus with her eyes brave Tamyris spake dread,
19. Which when the kings dull breast not entered,
20. Finding she could not looke, she strook him dead.
