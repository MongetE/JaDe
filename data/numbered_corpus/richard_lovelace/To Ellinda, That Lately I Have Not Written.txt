1. I.
2. If in me anger, or disdaine
3. In you, or both, made me refraine
4. From th' noble intercourse of verse,
5. That only vertuous thoughts rehearse;
6. Then, chaste Ellinda, might you feare
7. The sacred vowes that I did sweare.

8. II.
9. But if alone some pious thought
10. Me to an inward sadnesse brought,
11. Thinking to breath your soule too welle,
12. My tongue was charmed with that spell;
13. And left it (since there was no roome
14. To voyce your worth enough) strooke dumbe.

15. III.
16. So then this silence doth reveal
17. No thought of negligence, but zeal:
18. For, as in adoration,
19. This is love's true devotion;
20. Children and fools the words repeat,
21. But anch'rites pray in tears and sweat.
