1. AD FABULLUM.  CATUL. LIB. I. EP. 13.

2. Caenabis bene, mi Fabulle, apud me
3. Paucis, si dii tibi favent, diebus;
4. Si tecum attuleris bonam atque magnam
5. Caenam, non sine candida puella,
6. Et vino, et sale, et omnibus cachinnis.
7. Haec si, inquam, attuleris, Fabulle noster,
8. Caenabis bene: nam tui Catulli
9. Plenus sacculus est aranearum.
10. Sed, contra, accipies meros amores,
11. Seu quod suavius elegantiusve est:
12. Nam unguentum dabo, quod meae puellae
13. Donarunt Veneres Cupidinesque;
14. Quod tu cum olfacies, deos rogabis,
15. Totum te faciant, Fabulle, nasum.

16. ENGLISHED.

17. Fabullus, I will treat you handsomely
18. Shortly, if the kind gods will favour thee.
19. If thou dost bring with thee a del'cate messe,
20. An olio or so, a pretty lass,
21. Brisk wine, sharp tales, all sorts of drollery,
22. These if thou bringst (I say) along with thee,
23. You shall feed highly, friend: for, know, the ebbs
24. Of my lank purse are full of spiders webs;
25. But then again you shall receive clear love,
26. Or what more grateful or more sweet may prove:
27. For with an ointment I will favour thee
28. My Venus's and Cupids gave to me,
29. Of which once smelt, the gods thou wilt implore,
30. Fabullus, that they'd make thee nose all ore.
