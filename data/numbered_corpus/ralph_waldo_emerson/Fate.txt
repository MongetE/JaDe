1. Deep in the man sits fast his fate
2. To mould his fortunes, mean or great:
3. Unknown to Cromwell as to me
4. Was Cromwell's measure or degree;
5. Unknown to him as to his horse,
6. If he than his groom be better or worse.
7. He works, plots, fights, in rude affairs,
8. With squires, lords, kings, his craft compares,
9. Till late he learned, through doubt and fear,
10. Broad England harbored not his peer:
11. Obeying time, the last to own
12. The Genius from its cloudy throne.
13. For the prevision is allied
14. Unto the thing so signified;
15. Or say, the foresight that awaits
16. Is the same Genius that creates.
