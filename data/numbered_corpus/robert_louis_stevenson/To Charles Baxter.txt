1. OUR Johnie's deid.  The mair's the pity!
2. He's deid, an' deid o' Aqua-vitae.
3. O Embro', you're a shrunken city,
4. Noo Johnie's deid!
5. Tak hands, an' sing a burial ditty
6. Ower Johnie's heid.

7. To see him was baith drink an' meat,
8. Gaun linkin' glegly up the street.
9. He but to rin or tak a seat,
10. The wee bit body!
11. Bein' aye unsicken on his feet
12. Wi' whusky toddy.

13. To be aye tosh was Johnie's whim,
14. There's nane was better teut than him,
15. Though whiles his gravit-knot wad clim'
16. Ahint his ear,
17. An' whiles he'd buttons oot or in
18. The less ae mair.

19. His hair a' lang about his bree,
20. His tap-lip lang by inches three -
21. A slockened sort 'mon,' to pree
22. A' sensuality -
23. A droutly glint was in his e'e
24. An' personality.

25. An' day an' nicht, frae daw to daw,
26. Dink an' perjink an' doucely braw,
27. Wi' a kind o' Gospel ower a',
28. May or October,
29. Like Peden, followin' the Law
30. An' no that sober.

31. Whusky an' he were pack thegether.
32. Whate'er the hour, whate'er the weather,
33. John kept himsel' wi' mistened leather
34. An' kindled spunk.
35. Wi' him, there was nae askin' whether -
36. John was aye drunk.

37. The auncient heroes gash an' bauld
38. In the uncanny days of auld,
39. The task ance fo(u)nd to which th'were called,
40. Stack stenchly to it.
41. His life sic noble lives recalled,
42. Little's he knew it.

43. Single an' straucht, he went his way.
44. He kept the faith an' played the play.
45. Whusky an' he were man an' may
46. Whate'er betided.
47. Bonny in life - in death - this twae
48. Were no' divided.

49. An' wow! but John was unco sport.
50. Whiles he wad smile about the Court
51. Malvolio-like - whiles snore an' snort
52. Was heard afar.
53. The idle winter lads' resort
54. Was aye John's bar.

55. What's merely humorous or bonny
56. The Worl' regairds wi' cauld astony.
57. Drunk men tak' aye mair place than ony;
58. An' sae, ye see,
59. The gate was aye ower thrang for Johnie -
60. Or you an' me.

61. John micht hae jingled cap an' bells,
62. Been a braw fule in silks an' pells,
63. In ane o' the auld worl's canty hells
64. Paris or Sodom.
65. I wadnae had him naething else
66. But Johnie Adam.

67. He suffered - as have a' that wan
68. Eternal memory frae man,
69. Since e'er the weary worl' began -
70. Mister or Madam,
71. Keats or Scots Burns, the Spanish Don
72. Or Johnie Adam.

73. We leuch, an' Johnie deid.  An' fegs!
74. Hoo he had keept his stoiterin' legs
75. Sae lang's he did's a fact that begs
76. An explanation.
77. He stachers fifty years - syne plegs
78. To's destination.
