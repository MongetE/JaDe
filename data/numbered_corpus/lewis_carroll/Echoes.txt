1. Lady Clara Vere de Vere
2. Was eight years old, she said:
3. Every ringlet, lightly shaken, ran itself in golden thread.

4. She took her little porringer:
5. Of me she shall not win renown:
6. For the baseness of its nature shall have strength to drag her
7. down.

8. "Sisters and brothers, little Maid?
9. There stands the Inspector at thy door:
10. Like a dog, he hunts for boys who know not two and two are four."

11. "Kind words are more than coronets,"
12. She said, and wondering looked at me:
13. "It is the dead unhappy night, and I must hurry home to tea."
