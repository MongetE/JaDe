1. Merry voices chatterin',
2. Nimble feet dem patterin',
3. Big an' little, faces gay,
4. Happy day dis market day.

5. Sateday, de marnin' break,
6. Soon, soon market-people wake;
7. An' de light shine from de moon
8. While dem boy, wid pantaloon
9. Roll up ober dem knee-pan,
10. 'Tep across de buccra lan'
11. To de pastur whe' de harse
12. Feed along wid de jackass,
13. An' de mule cant' in de track
14. Wid him tail up in him back,
15. All de ketchin' to defy,
16. No ca' how dem boy might try.

17. In de early marnin'-tide,
18. When de cocks crow on de hill
19. An' de stars are shinin' still,
20. Mirrie by de fireside
21. Hots de coffee for de lads
22. Comin' ridin' on de pads
23. T'rown across dem animul--
24. Donkey, harse too, an' de mule,
25. Which at last had come do'n cool.
26. On de bit dem hol' dem full:
27. Racin' ober pastur' lan',
28. See dem comin' ebery man,
29. Comin' fe de steamin' tea
30. Ober hilly track an' lea.

31. Hard-wuk'd donkey on de road
32. Trottin' wid him ushal load,
33. Hamper pack' wi' yam an' grain,
34. Sour-sop, and Gub'nor cane.

35. Cous' Sun sits in hired dray,
36. Drivin' 'long de market way;
37. Whole week grindin' sugar cane
38. T'rough de boilin' sun an' rain,
39. Now, a'ter de toilin' hard,
40. He goes seekin' his reward,
41. While he's thinkin' in him min'
42. Of de dear ones lef behin',
43. Of de loved though ailin' wife,
44. Darlin' treasure of his life,
45. An' de picknies, six in all,
46. Whose 'nuff burdens 'pon him fall:
47. Seben lovin' ones in need,
48. Seben hungry mouths fe feed;
49. On deir wants he thinks alone,
50. Neber dreamin' of his own,
51. But gwin' on wid joyful face
52. Till him re'ch de market-place.

53. Sugar bears no price to-day,
54. Though it is de mont' o' May,
55. When de time is hellish hot,
56. An' de water cocoanut
57. An' de cane bebridge is nice,
58. Mix' up wid a lilly ice.
59. Big an' little, great an' small,
60. Afou yam is all de call;
61. Sugar tup an' gill a quart,
62. Yet de people hab de heart
63. Wantin' brater top o' i',
64. Want de sweatin' higgler fe
65. Ram de pan an' pile i' up,
66. Yet sell i' fe so-so tup.

67. Cousin Sun is lookin' sad,
68. As de market is so bad;
69. 'Pon him han' him res' him chin,
70. Quietly sit do'n thinkin'
71. Of de loved wife sick in bed,
72. An' de children to be fed--
73. What de laborers would say
74. When dem know him couldn' pay;
75. Also what about de mill
76. Whe' him hire from ole Bill;
77. So him think, an' think on so,
78. Till him t'oughts no more could go.

79. Then he got up an' began
80. Pickin' up him sugar-pan:
81. In his ears rang t'rough de din
82. 'Only two-an'-six a tin'.'
83. What a tale he'd got to tell,
84. How bad, bad de sugar sell!
85. Tekin' out de lee amount,
86. Him set do'n an' begin count
87. All de time him min' deh doubt
88. How expenses would pay out;
89. Ah, it gnawed him like de ticks,
90. Sugar sell fe two-an'-six!

91. So he journeys on de way,
92. Feelinl sad dis market day;
93. No e'en buy a little cake
94. To gi'e baby when she wake,--
95. Passin' 'long de candy-shop
96. 'Douten eben mek a stop
97. To buy drops fe las'y son,
98. For de lilly cash nea' done.
99. So him re'ch him own a groun',
100. An' de children scamper roun',
101. Each one stretchin' out him han',
102. Lookin' to de poor sad man.

103. Oh, how much he felt de blow,
104. As he watched dem face fall low,
105. When dem wait an' nuttin' came
106. An' drew back deir han's wid shame!
107. But de sick wife kissed his brow:
108. 'Sun, don't get down-hearted now;
109. Ef we only pay expense
110. We mus' wuk we common-sense,
111. Cut an' carve, an' carve an' cut,
112. Mek gill sarbe fe quattiewut;
113. We mus' try mek two ends meet
114. Neber mind how hard be it.
115. We won't mind de haul an' pull,
116. While dem pickny belly full.'

117. An' de shadow lef' him face,
118. An' him felt an inward peace,
119. As he blessed his better part
120. For her sweet an' gentle heart:
121. 'Dear one o' my heart, my breat',
122. Won't I lub you to de deat'?
123. When my heart is weak an' sad,
124. Who but you can mek it glad?'

125. So dey kissed an' kissed again,
126. An' deir t'oughts were not on pain,
127. But was 'way down in de sout'
128. Where dey'd wedded in deir yout',
129. In de marnin' of deir life
130. Free from all de grief an' strife,
131. Happy in de marnin' light,
132. Never thinkin' of de night.

133. So dey k'lated eberyt'ing;
134. An' de profit it could bring,
135. A'ter all de business fix',
136. Was a princely two-an'-six.
