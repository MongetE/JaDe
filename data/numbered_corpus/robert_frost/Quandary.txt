1. Never have I been glad or sad
2. That there was such a thing as bad.
3. There had to be, I understood,
4. For there to have been any good.
5. It was by having been contrasted
6. That good and bad so long had lasted.
7. That's why discrimination reigns.
8. That's why we need a lot of brains
9. If only to discriminate
10. 'Twixt what to love and what to hate.
11. To quote the oracle at Delphi,
12. Love thy neighbor as thyself, aye,
13. And hate him as thyself thou hatest.
14. There quandary is at its greatest.
15. We learned from the forbidden fruit
16. For brains there is no substitute.
17. 'Unless it's sweetbreads, ' you suggest
18. With innuendo I detest.
19. You drive me to confess in ink:
20. Once I was fool enough to think
21. That brains and sweetbreads were the same,
22. Till I was caught and put to shame,
23. First by a butcher, then a cook,
24. Then by a scientific book.
25. But ' twas by making sweetbreads do
26. I passed with such a high I.Q.
