1. Here in this dim, dull, double-bedded room,
2. I play the father to a brace of boys,
3. Ailing but apt for every sort of noise,
4. Bedfast but brilliant yet with health and bloom.
5. Roden, the Irishman, is 'sieven past,'
6. Blue-eyed, snub-nosed, chubby, and fair of face.
7. Willie's but six, and seems to like the place,
8. A cheerful little collier to the last.
9. They eat, and laugh, and sing, and fight, all day;
10. All night they sleep like dormice. See them play
11. At Operations:- Roden, the Professor,
12. Saws, lectures, takes the artery up, and ties;
13. Willie, self-chloroformed, with half-shut eyes,
14. Holding the limb and moaning-Case and Dresser.
