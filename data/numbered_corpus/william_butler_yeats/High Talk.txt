1. PROCESSIONS that lack high stilts have nothing that catches the eye.
2. What if my great-granddad had a pair that were twenty foot high,
3. And mine were but fifteen foot, no modern Stalks upon higher,
4. Some rogue of the world stole them to patch up a fence or a fire.
5. Because piebald ponies, led bears, caged lions, ake but poor shows,
6. Because children demand Daddy-long-legs upon This timber toes,
7. Because women in the upper storeys demand a face at the pane,
8. That patching old heels they may shriek, I take to chisel and plane.

9. Malachi Stilt-Jack am I, whatever I learned has run wild, From collar to collar, from stilt to stilt, from father to child.
10. All metaphor, Malachi, stilts and all.  A barnacle goose
11. Far up in the stretches of night; night splits and the dawn breaks loose;
12. I, through the terrible novelty of light, stalk on, stalk on;
13. Those great sea-horses bare their teeth and laugh at the dawn.
