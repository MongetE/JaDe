1. 'I am of Ireland,
2. And the Holy Land of Ireland,
3. And time runs on,' cried she.
4. 'Come out of charity,
5. Come dance with me in Ireland.'

6. One man, one man alone
7. In that outlandish gear,
8. One solitary man
9. Of all that rambled there
10. Had turned his stately head.
11. That is a long way off,
12. And time runs on,' he said,
13. 'And the night grows rough.'

14. 'I am of Ireland,
15. And the Holy Land of Ireland,
16. And time runs on,' cried she.
17. 'Come out of charity
18. And dance with me in Ireland.'
19. 'The fiddlers are all thumbs,
20. Or the fiddle-string accursed,
21. The drums and the kettledrums
22. And the trumpets all are burst,
23. And the trombone,' cried he,
24. 'The trumpet and trombone,'
25. And cocked a malicious eye,
26. 'But time runs on, runs on.'

27. I am of Ireland,
28. And the Holy Land of Ireland,
29. And time runs on,' cried she.
30. "Come out of charity
31. And dance with me in Ireland.'
