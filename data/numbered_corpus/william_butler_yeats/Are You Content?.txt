1. I CALL on those that call me son,
2. Grandson, or great-grandson,
3. On uncles, aunts, great-uncles or great-aunts,
4. To judge what I have done.
5. Have I, that put it into words,
6. Spoilt what old loins have sent?
7. Eyes spiritualised by death can judge,
8. I cannot, but I am not content.
9. He that in Sligo at Drumcliff
10. Set up the old stone Cross,
11. That red-headed rector in County Down,
12. A good man on a horse,
13. Sandymount Corbets, that notable man
14. Old William pollexfen,
15. The smuggler Middleton, Butlers far back,
16. Half legendary men.
17. Infirm and aged I might stay
18. In some good company,
19. I who have always hated work,
20. Smiling at the sea,
21. Or demonstrate in my own life
22. What Robert Browning meant
23. By an old hunter talking with Gods;
24. But I am not content.
