1. WOULD it were anything but merely voice!'
2. The No King cried who after that was King,
3. Because he had not heard of anything
4. That balanced with a word is more than noise;
5. Yet Old Romance being kind, let him prevail
6. Somewhere or somehow that I have forgot,
7. Though he'd but cannon -- Whereas we that had thought
8. To have lit upon as clean and sweet a tale
9. Have been defeated by that pledge you gave
10. In momentary anger long ago;
11. And I that have not your faith, how shall I know
12. That in the blinding light beyond the grave
13. We'll find so good a thing as that we have lost?
14. The hourly kindness, the day's common speech.
15. The habitual content of each with each
16. Men neither soul nor body has been crossed.
