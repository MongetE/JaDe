1. I
2. LEST it may more quarrels breed,
3. I will never hear you read.

4. II
5. By disputing, I will never,
6. To convince you once endeavour.

7. III
8. When a paradox you stick to,
9. I will never contradict you.

10. IV
11. When I talk and you are heedless,
12. I will show no anger needless.

13. V
14. When your speeches are absurd,
15. I will ne'er object a word.

16. VI
17. When you furious argue wrong,
18. I will grieve and hold my tongue.

19. VII
20. Not a jest or humorous story
21. Will I ever tell before ye:
22. To be chidden for explaining,
23. When you quite mistake the meaning.

24. VIII
25. Never more will I suppose,
26. You can taste my verse or prose.

27. IX
28. You no more at me shall fret,
29. While I teach and you forget.

30. X
31. You shall never hear me thunder,
32. When you blunder on, and blunder.

33. XI
34. Show your poverty of spirit,
35. And in dress place all your merit;
36. Give yourself ten thousand airs:
37. That with me shall break no squares.

38. XII
39. Never will I give advice,
40. Till you please to ask me thrice:
41. Which if you in scorn reject,
42. 'Twill be just as I expect.

43. Thus we both shall have our ends,
44. And continue special friends.
