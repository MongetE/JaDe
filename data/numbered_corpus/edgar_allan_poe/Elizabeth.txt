1. Elizabeth, it surely is most fit
2. [Logic and common usage so commanding]
3. In thy own book that first thy name be writ,
4. Zeno and other sages notwithstanding;
5. And I have other reasons for so doing
6. Besides my innate love of contradiction;
7. Each poet - if a poet - in pursuing
8. The muses thro' their bowers of Truth or Fiction,
9. Has studied very little of his part,
10. Read nothing, written less - in short's a fool
11. Endued with neither soul, nor sense, nor art,
12. Being ignorant of one important rule,
13. Employed in even the theses of the school-
14. Called - I forget the heathenish Greek name
15. [Called anything, its meaning is the same]
16. "Always write first things uppermost in the heart."
