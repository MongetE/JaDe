1. I

2. But our Great Turks in wit must reign alone
3. And ill can bear a Brother on the Throne.


4. II

5. Wit is like faith by such warm Fools profest
6. Who to be saved by one, must damn the rest.


7. III

8. Some who grow dull religious strait commence
9. And gain in morals what they lose in sence.


10. IV

11. Wits starve as useless to a Common weal
12. While Fools have places purely for their Zea.

13. V

14. Now wits gain praise by copying other wits
15. As one Hog lives on what another sh---.


16. VI

17. Wou'd you your writings to some Palates fit
18. Purged all you verses from the sin of wit
19. For authors now are so conceited grown
20. They praise no works but what are like their own.
