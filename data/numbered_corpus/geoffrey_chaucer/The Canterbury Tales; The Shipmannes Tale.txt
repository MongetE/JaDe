1. Part 8

2. PROLOGUE TO THE SHIPMANNES TALE

3. Here endith the man of lawe his tale. And next folwith
4. the Shipman his prolog.

5. Oure Ost upon his stiropes stood anoon,
6. And seide, 'Good men, herkeneth everychoon;
7. This was a thrifty tale for the nonys.

8. Sir parisshe preste,' quod he, 'for Godis bonys,
9. Telle us a tale, as was thi forward yore;
10. I se wel, that ye lernede men in lore
11. Can meche good, bi Godis dignite.'
12. The parson him answerde, 'Benedicite,

13. What eyleth the man so synfully to swere?'
14. Oure Ost answerde, 'O Jankyn, be ye there?
15. I smelle a Lollere in the wynde,' quod he,
16. 'Howe, goodmen,' quod oure Hoste, 'herkeneth me,
17. Abyde for Godis digne passioun,

18. For we shul han a predicacioun,
19. This Lollere here wol prechen us somwhat.'
20. 'Nay, bi Godis soule, that shal he nat,'
21. Seyde the Shipman, 'here shal he not preche,
22. He shal no gospel glosen here, ne teche.

23. We leven alle in the grete God,' quod he,
24. 'He wolde sowen som difficulte
25. Or sprengen cokkel in oure clene corn.
26. And therfore, Ost, I warne the biforn,
27. My joly body shal a tale telle

28. And I shal clynkyn yow so mery a belle
29. That I shal wakyn al this companye;
30. But it shal not ben of Philosophie,
31. Ne phislyas, ne termes queynte of lawe;
32. Ther nis but litil Latyn in my mawe.'

33. Here endith the Shipman his prolog. And next folwyng
34. he bigynneth his tale.

35. THE TALE.

36. (Daun John, a monk of Paris, beguiles the wife of a
37. merchant of St. Denis by money borrowed from her husband.
38. She saves herself, on the point of discovery, by a ready
39. answer.)

40. END-LINK

41. Bihoold the murie wordes of the Hoost to the Shipman
42. and to the lady Prioresse.

43. 'Wel seyd, by corpus dominus,' quod our Hoost,
44. 'Now longe moote thou saille by the cost,
45. Sir gentil maister, gentil maryneer.
46. God yeve this monk a thousand last quade yeer!
47. A ha! felawes, beth ware of swich a jape.

48. The monk putte in the mannes hood an ape,
49. And in his wyves eek, by Seint Austyn;
50. Draweth no monkes moore unto your in.
51. But now passe over, and lat us seke aboute
52. Who shal now telle first of al this route

53. Another tale?' and with that word he sayde,
54. As curteisly as it had ben a mayde,
55. 'My lady Prioresse, by youre leve,
56. So that I wiste I sholde yow nat greve,
57. I wolde demen that ye tellen sholde

58. A tale next, if so were that ye wolde.
59. Now wol ye vouchesauf, my lady deere?'
60. 'Gladly,' quod she, and seyde as ye shal heere.
