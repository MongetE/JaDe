1. Oh, "rorty" was a mid-Victorian word
2. Which meant "fine, splendid, jolly,"
3. And often to me it has reoccurred
4. In moments melancholy.
5. For instance, children, I think it rorty
6. To be with people over forty.

7. I can't say which, come eventide,
8. More tedious I find;
9. Competing with the juvenile stride,
10. Or meeting the juvenile mind.
11. So I think it rorty, yes, and nifty,
12. To be with people over fifty.

13. The pidgin talk the youthful use
14. Bypasses conversation.
15. I can't believe the code they choose
16. Is a means of communication.
17. Oh to be with people over sixty
18. Despite their tendency to prolixty!

19. The hours a working parent keeps
20. Mean less than Latin to them,
21. Wherefore they disappear in jeeps
22. Till three and four A.M.
23. Oh, to be with people you pour a cup for
24. Instead of people you have to wait up for!

25. I've tried to read young mumbling lips
26. Till I've developed a slant-eye,
27. And my hearing fails at the constant wails
28. Of, If I can't, why can't I?
29. Oh, to be beside a septuagenarian,
30. Silent upon a peak in Darien!

31. They don't know Hagen from Bobby Jones,
32. They never heard of Al Smith,
33. Even Red Grange is beyond their range,
34. And Dempsey is a myth.
35. Oh golly, to gabble upon the shoulder
36. Of someone my own age, or even older!

37. I'm tired of defining hadn't oughts.
38. To opposition mulish,
39. The thoughts of youth are long long thoughts,
40. And Jingo! Aren't they foolish!
41. All which is why, in case you've wondered
42. I'd like a companion aged one hundred.
