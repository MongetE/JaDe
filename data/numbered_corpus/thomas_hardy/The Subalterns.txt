1. I

2. "Poor wanderer," said the leaden sky,
3. "I fain would lighten thee,
4. But there are laws in force on high
5. Which say it must not be."

6. II

7. --"I would not freeze thee, shorn one," cried
8. The North, "knew I but how
9. To warm my breath, to slack my stride;
10. But I am ruled as thou."

11. III

12. --"To-morrow I attack thee, wight,"
13. Said Sickness. "Yet I swear
14. I bear thy little ark no spite,
15. But am bid enter there."

16. IV

17. --"Come hither, Son," I heard Death say;
18. "I did not will a grave
19. Should end thy pilgrimage to-day,
20. But I, too, am a slave!"

21. V

22. We smiled upon each other then,
23. And life to me had less
24. Of that fell look it wore ere when
25. They owned their passiveness.
