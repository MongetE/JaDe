1. I

2. There dwells a mighty pair -
3. Slow, statuesque, intense -
4. Amid the vague Immense:
5. None can their chronicle declare,
6. Nor why they be, nor whence.

7. ,h II

8. Mother of all things made,
9. Matchless in artistry,
10. Unlit with sight is she. -
11. And though her ever well-obeyed
12. Vacant of feeling he.

13. III

14. The Matron mildly asks -
15. A throb in every word -
16. "Our clay-made creatures, lord,
17. How fare they in their mortal tasks
18. Upon Earth's bounded bord?

19. IV

20. "The fate of those I bear,
21. Dear lord, pray turn and view,
22. And notify me true;
23. Shapings that eyelessly I dare
24. Maybe I would undo.

25. V

26. "Sometimes from lairs of life
27. Methinks I catch a groan,
28. Or multitudinous moan,
29. As though I had schemed a world of strife,
30. Working by touch alone."

31. VI

32. "World-weaver!he replies,
33. "I scan all thy domain;
34. But since nor joy nor pain
35. Doth my clear substance recognize,
36. I read thy realms in vain.

37. VII

38. "World-weaver! what IS Grief?
39. And what are Right, and Wrong,
40. And Feeling, that belong
41. To creatures all who owe thee fief?
42. What worse is Weak than Strong?" . . .

43. VIII

44. --Unlightened, curious, meek,
45. She broods in sad surmise . . .
46. --Some say they have heard her sighs
47. On Alpine height or Polar peak
48. When the night tempests rise.
