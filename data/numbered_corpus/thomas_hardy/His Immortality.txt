1. I

2. I saw a dead man's finer part
3. Shining within each faithful heart
4. Of those bereft. Then said I: "This must be
5. His immortality."

6. II

7. I looked there as the seasons wore,
8. And still his soul continuously upbore
9. Its life in theirs. But less its shine excelled
10. Than when I first beheld.

11. III

12. His fellow-yearsmen passed, and then
13. In later hearts I looked for him again;
14. And found him--shrunk, alas! into a thin
15. And spectral mannikin.

16. IV

17. Lastly I ask--now old and chill -
18. If aught of him remain unperished still;
19. And find, in me alone, a feeble spark,
20. Dying amid the dark.
