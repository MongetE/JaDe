1. I
2. I heard a small sad sound,
3. And stood awhile among the tombs around:
4. "Wherefore, old friends," said I, "are you distrest,
5. Now, screened from life's unrest?"

6. II
7. --"O not at being here;
8. But that our future second death is near;
9. When, with the living, memory of us numbs,
10. And blank oblivion comes!

11. III
12. "These, our sped ancestry,
13. Lie here embraced by deeper death than we;
14. Nor shape nor thought of theirs can you descry
15. With keenest backward eye.

16. IV
17. "They count as quite forgot;
18. They are as men who have existed not;
19. Theirs is a loss past loss of fitful breath;
20. It is the second death.

21. V
22. "We here, as yet, each day
23. Are blest with dear recall; as yet, can say
24. We hold in some soul loved continuance
25. Of shape and voice and glance.

26. VI
27. "But what has been will be --
28. First memory, then oblivion's swallowing sea;
29. Like men foregone, shall we merge into those
30. Whose story no one knows.

31. VII
32. "For which of us could hope
33. To show in life that world-awakening scope
34. Granted the few whose memory none lets die,
35. But all men magnify?

36. VIII
37. "We were but Fortune's sport;
38. Things true, things lovely, things of good report
39. We neither shunned nor sought ... We see our bourne,
40. And seeing it we mourn."
