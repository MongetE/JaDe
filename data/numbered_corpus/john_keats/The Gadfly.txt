1. 1.
2. All gentle folks who owe a grudge
3. To any living thing
4. Open your ears and stay your t[r]udge
5. Whilst I in dudgeon sing.

6. 2.
7. The Gadfly he hath stung me sore--
8. O may he ne'er sting you!
9. But we have many a horrid bore
10. He may sting black and blue.

11. 3.
12. Has any here an old grey Mare
13. With three legs all her store,
14. O put it to her Buttocks bare
15. And straight she'll run on four.

16. 4.
17. Has any here a Lawyer suit
18. Of 1743,
19. Take Lawyer's nose and put it to't
20. And you the end will see.

21. 5.
22. Is there a Man in Parliament
23. Dum[b-] founder'd in his speech,
24. O let his neighbour make a rent
25. And put one in his breech.

26. 6.
27. O Lowther how much better thou
28. Hadst figur'd t'other day
29. When to the folks thou mad'st a bow
30. And hadst no more to say.

31. 7.
32. If lucky Gadfly had but ta'en
33. His seat * * * * * * * * *
34. And put thee to a little pain
35. To save thee from a worse.

36. 8.
37. Better than Southey it had been,
38. Better than Mr. D-------,
39. Better than Wordsworth too, I ween,
40. Better than Mr. V-------.

41. 9.
42. Forgive me pray good people all
43. For deviating so --
44. In spirit sure I had a call --
45. And now I on will go.

46. 10.
47. Has any here a daughter fair
48. Too fond of reading novels,
49. Too apt to fall in love with care
50. And charming Mister Lovels,

51. 11.
52. O put a Gadfly to that thing
53. She keeps so white and pert --
54. I mean the finger for the ring,
55. And it will breed a wort.

56. 12.
57. Has any here a pious spouse
58. Who seven times a day
59. Scolds as King David pray'd, to chouse
60. And have her holy way --

61. 13.
62. O let a Gadfly's little sting
63. Persuade her sacred tongue
64. That noises are a common thing,
65. But that her bell has rung.
