1. 1.
2. Can death be sleep, when life is but a dream,
3. And scenes of bliss pass as a phantom by?
4. The transient pleasures as a vision seem,
5. And yet we think the greatest pain's to die.

6. 2.
7. How strange it is that man on earth should roam,
8. And lead a life of woe, but not forsake
9. His rugged path; nor dare he view alone
10. His future doom which is but to awake.
