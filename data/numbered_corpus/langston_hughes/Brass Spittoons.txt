1. Clean the spittoons, boy.
2. Detroit,
3. Chicago,
4. Atlantic City,
5. Palm Beach.
6. Clean the spittoons.
7. The steam in hotel kitchens,
8. And the smoke in hotel lobbies,
9. And the slime in hotel spittoons:
10. Part of my life.
11. Hey, boy!
12. A nickel,
13. A dime,
14. A dollar,
15. Two dollars a day.
16. Hey, boy!
17. A nickel,
18. A dime,
19. A dollar,
20. Two dollars
21. Buy shoes for the baby.
22. House rent to pay.
23. Gin on Saturday,
24. Church on Sunday.
25. My God!
26. Babies and gin and church
27. And women and Sunday
28. All mixed with dimes and
29. Dollars and clean spittoons
30. And house rent to pay.
31. Hey, boy!
32. A bright bowl of brass is beautiful to the Lord.
33. Bright polished brass like the cymbals
34. Of King David’s dancers,
35. Like the wine cups of Solomon.
36. Hey, boy!
37. A clean spittoon on the altar of the Lord.
38. A clean bright spittoon all newly polished—
39. At least I can offer that.
40. Com’mere, boy!
