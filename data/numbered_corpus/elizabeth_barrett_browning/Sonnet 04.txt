1. IV

2. Thou hast thy calling to some palace-floor,
3. Most gracious singer of high poems! where
4. The dancers will break footing, from the care
5. Of watching up thy pregnant lips for more.
6. And dost thou lift this house's latch too poor
7. For hand of thine? and canst thou think and bear
8. To let thy music drop here unaware
9. In folds of golden fulness at my door?
10. Look up and see the casement broken in,
11. The bats and owlets builders in the roof!
12. My cricket chirps against thy mandolin.
13. Hush, call no echo up in further proof
14. Of desolation! there 's a voice within
15. That weeps . . . as thou must sing . . . alone, aloof
