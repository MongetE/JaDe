1. THEY shall arise in the States,
2. They shall report Nature, laws, physiology, and happiness;
3. They shall illustrate Democracy and the kosmos;
4. They shall be alimentive, amative, perceptive;
5. They shall be complete women and men--their pose brawny and supple,
6. their drink water, their blood clean and clear;
7. They shall enjoy materialism and the sight of products--they shall
8. enjoy the sight of the beef, lumber, bread-stuffs, of Chicago,
9. the great city;
10. They shall train themselves to go in public to become orators and
11. oratresses;
12. Strong and sweet shall their tongues be--poems and materials of poems
13. shall come from their lives--they shall be makers and finders;
14. Of them, and of their works, shall emerge divine conveyers, to convey
15. gospels;
16. Characters, events, retrospections, shall be convey'd in gospels
17. --Trees, animals, waters, shall be convey'd,
18. Death, the future, the invisible faith, shall all be convey'd.
