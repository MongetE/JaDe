1. WHO includes diversity, and is Nature,
2. Who is the amplitude of the earth, and the coarseness and sexuality
3. of the earth, and the great charity of the earth, and the
4. equilibrium also,
5. Who has not look'd forth from the windows, the eyes, for nothing, or
6. whose brain held audience with messengers for nothing;
7. Who contains believers and disbelievers--Who is the most majestic
8. lover;
9. Who holds duly his or her triune proportion of realism, spiritualism,
10. and of the aesthetic, or intellectual,
11. Who, having consider'd the Body, finds all its organs and parts good;
12. Who, out of the theory of the earth, and of his or her body,
13. understands by subtle analogies all other theories,
14. The theory of a city, a poem, and of the large politics of These
15. States;
16. Who believes not only in our globe, with its sun and moon, but in
17. other globes, with their suns and moons;
18. Who, constructing the house of himself or herself, not for a day, but
19. for all time, sees races, eras, dates, generations,
20. The past, the future, dwelling there, like space, inseparable
21. together.
