1. WHEN I read the book, the biography famous,
2. And is this, then, (said I,) what the author calls a man's life?
3. And so will some one, when I am dead and gone, write my life?
4. (As if any man really knew aught of my life;
5. Why, even I myself, I often think, know little or nothing of my real
6. life;
7. Only a few hints--a few diffused, faint clues and indirections,
8. I seek, for my own use, to trace out here.)
