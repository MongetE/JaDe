1. ME imperturbe, standing at ease in Nature,
2. Master of all, or mistress of all--aplomb in the midst of irrational
3. things,
4. Imbued as they--passive, receptive, silent as they,
5. Finding my occupation, poverty, notoriety, foibles, crimes, less
6. important than I thought;
7. Me private, or public, or menial, or solitary--all these subordinate,
8. (I am eternally equal with the best--I am not subordinate;)
9. Me toward the Mexican Sea, or in the Mannahatta, or the Tennessee, or
10. far north, or inland,
11. A river man, or a man of the woods, or of any farm-life in These
12. States, or of the coast, or the lakes, or Kanada,
13. Me, wherever my life is lived, O to be
14. self-balanced for
15. contingencies!
16. O to confront night, storms, hunger, ridicule, accidents, rebuffs, as
17. the trees and animals do.
