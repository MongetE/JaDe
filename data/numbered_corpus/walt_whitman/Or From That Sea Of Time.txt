1. OR, from that Sea of Time,
2. Spray, blown by the wind--a double winrow-drift of weeds and shells;
3. (O little shells, so curious-convolute! so limpid-cold and voiceless!
4. Yet will you not, to the tympans of temples held,
5. Murmurs and echoes still bring up--Eternity's music, faint and far,
6. Wafted inland, sent from Atlantica's rim--strains for the Soul of the
7. Prairies,
8. Whisper'd reverberations--chords for the ear of the West, joyously
9. sounding
10. Your tidings old, yet ever new and untranslatable;)
11. Infinitessimals out of my life, and many a life,
12. (For not my life and years alone I give--all, all I give;)
13. These thoughts and Songs--waifs from the deep--here, cast high and
14. dry,
15. Wash'd on America's shores.


16. Currents of starting a Continent new,
17. Overtures sent to the solid out of the liquid,
18. Fusion of ocean and land--tender and pensive waves,
19. (Not safe and peaceful only--waves rous'd and ominous too.
20. Out of the depths, the storm's abysms--Who knows whence? Death's
21. waves,
22. Raging over the vast, with many a broken spar and tatter'd sail.)
