1. AS if a phantom caress'd me,
2. I thought I was not alone, walking here by the shore;
3. But the one I thought was with me, as now I walk by the shore--the
4. one I loved, that caress'd me,
5. As I lean and look through the glimmering light--that one has utterly
6. disappear'd,
7. And those appear that are hateful to me, and mock me.
