1. A was an ant
2. Who seldom stood still,
3. And who made a nice house
4. In the side of a hill.
5. Nice little ant!

6. B was a bat,
7. Who slept all the day,
8. And fluttered about
9. When the sun went away.
10. Brown little bat!

11. C was a camel:
12. You rode on his hump;
13. And if you fell off,
14. You came down such a bump!
15. What a high camel!

16. D was a duck
17. With spots on his back,
18. Who lived in the water,
19. And always said 'Quack!'
20. Dear little duck!

21. E was an elephant,
22. Stately and wise:
23. He had tusks and a trunk,
24. And two queer little eyes.
25. Oh, what funny small eyes!

26. F was a fish
27. Who was caught in a net;
28. But he got out again,
29. And is quite alive yet.
30. Lively young fish!

31. G was a goat
32. Who was spotted with brown:
33. When he did not lie still
34. He walked up and down.
35. Good little goat!

36. H was a hat
37. Which was all on one side;
38. Its crown was too high,
39. And its brim was too wide.
40. Oh, what a hat!

41. I was some ice
42. So white and so nice,
43. But which nobody tasted;
44. And so it was wasted.
45. All that good ice!

46. J was a jug,
47. So pretty and white,
48. With fresh water in it
49. At morning and night.
50. Nice little jug!

51. K was a kite
52. Which flew out of sight,
53. Above houses so high,
54. Quite into the sky.
55. Fly away, kite!
56. L was a lily,
57. So white and so sweet!
58. To see it and smell it
59. Was quite a nice treat.
60. Beautiful lily!

61. M was a man,
62. Who walked round and round;
63. And he wore a long coat
64. That came down to the ground.
65. Funny old man!

66. N was a net
67. Which was thrown In the sea
68. To catch fish for dinner
69. For you and for me.
70. Nice little net!

71. O was an orange
72. So yellow and round:
73. When it fell off the tree,
74. It fell down to the ground.
75. Down to the ground!

76. P was a polly.
77. All red, blue, and green,--
78. The most beautiful polly
79. That ever was seen.
80. Poor little polly!
81. Q was a quail
82. With a very short tail;
83. And he fed upon corn
84. In the evening and morn.
85. Quaint little quail!

86. R was a rabbit,
87. Who had a bad habit
88. Of eating the flowers
89. In gardens and bowers.
90. Naughty fat rabbit!

91. S was the sugar-tongs,
92. Nippity-nee,
93. To take up the sugar
94. To put in our tea.
95. Nippity-nee!

96. T was a tortoise,
97. All yellow and black:
98. He walked slowly away,
99. And he never came back.
100. Torty never came back!

101. U was an urn
102. All polished and bright,
103. And full of hot water
104. At noon and at night.
105. Useful old urn!

106. V was a veil
107. With a border upon it,
108. And a ribbon to tie it
109. All round a pink bonnet.
110. Pretty green veil!

111. W was a watch,
112. Where, in letters of gold,
113. The hour of the day
114. You might always behold.
115. Beautiful watch!

116. Y was a yew,
117. Which flourished and grew
118. By a quiet abode
119. Near the side of a road.
120. Dark little yew!

121. Z was a zebra,
122. All striped white and black;
123. And if he were tame,
124. You might ride on his back.
125. Pretty striped zebra!
