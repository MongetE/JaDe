1. Her grave, sweet haughtiness
2. Pleaseth me, and in like wise
3. Her quiet ironies.
4. Others are beautiful, none more, some less.

5. I suppose, when poetry comes down to facts,
6. When our souls are returned to the gods
7. And the spheres they belong in,
8. Here in the every-day where our acts
9. Rise up and judge us;

10. I suppose there are a few dozen verities
11. That no shift of mood can shake from us:

12. One place where we'd rather have tea
13. (Thus far hath modernity brought us)
14. 'Tea' (Damn you!)

15. Have tea, damn the Caesars,
16. Talk of the latest success, give wing to some scandal,
17. Garble a name we detest, and for prejudice?
18. Set loose the whole consummate pack
19. to bay like Sir Roger de Coverley's
20. This our reward for our works,
21. sic crescit gloria mundi:
22. Some circle of not more than three
23. that we prefer to play up to,
24. Some few whom we'd rather please
25. than hear the whole aegrum vulgus
26. Splitting its beery jowl
27. a-meaowling our praises.
28. Some certain peculiar things,
29. cari laresque, penates,
30. Some certain accustomed forms,
31. the absolute unimportant.
