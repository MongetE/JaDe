1. With Usura

2. With usura hath no man a house of good stone
3. each block cut smooth and well fitting
4. that design might cover their face,
5. with usura
6. hath no man a painted paradise on his church wall
7. harpes et luz
8. or where virgin receiveth message
9. and halo projects from incision,
10. with usura
11. seeth no man Gonzaga his heirs and his concubines
12. no picture is made to endure nor to live with
13. but it is made to sell and sell quickly
14. with usura, sin against nature,
15. is thy bread ever more of stale rags
16. is thy bread dry as paper,
17. with no mountain wheat, no strong flour
18. with usura the line grows thick
19. with usura is no clear demarcation
20. and no man can find site for his dwelling.
21. Stonecutter is kept from his tone
22. weaver is kept from his loom

23. WITH USURA
24. wool comes not to market
25. sheep bringeth no gain with usura

37. Usura rusteth the chisel

41. Azure hath a canker by usura; cramoisi is unbroidered
42. Emerald findeth no Memling
43. Usura slayeth the child in the womb
44. It stayeth the young man's courting
45. It hath brought palsey to bed, lyeth

47. CONTRA NATURAM
48. They have brought whores for Eleusis
49. Corpses are set to banquet
50. at behest of usura.



51. N.B. Usury: A charge for the use of purchasing power, levied without regard to production; often without regard to the possibilities of production. (Hence the failure of the Medici bank.)
