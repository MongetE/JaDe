1. The pomps of butchery, financial power,
2. Told 'em to die in war, and then to save,
3. Then cut their saving to the half or lower;
4. When will this system lie down in its grave?

5. The pomps of Fleet St., festering year on year,
6. Hid truth and lied, and lied and hid the facts.
7. The pimps of Whitehall ever more in fear,
8. Hid health statistics, dodged the Labour Acts.

9. All drew their pay, and as the pay grew less,
10. The money rotten and more rotten yet,
11. Hid more statistics, more feared to confess
12. C.3, C.4, 'twere better to forget

13. How many weak of mind, how much tuberculosis
14. Filled the back alleys and the back to back houses.

17. Time for that question? and the time is NOW.
