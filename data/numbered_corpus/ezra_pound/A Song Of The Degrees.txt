1. I
2. Rest me with Chinese colours,
3. For I think the glass is evil.

4. II
5. The wind moves above the wheat-
6. With a silver crashing,
7. A thin war of metal.

8. I have known the golden disc,
9. I have seen it melting above me.
10. I have known the stone-bright place,
11. The hall of clear colours.

12. III
13. O glass subtly evil, O confusion of colours !
14. O light bound and bent in, soul of the captive,
15. Why am I warned? Why am I sent away?
16. Why is your glitter full of curious mistrust?
17. O glass subtle and cunning, O powdery gold!
18. O filaments of amber, two-faced iridescence!
