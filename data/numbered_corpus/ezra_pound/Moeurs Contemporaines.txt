2. Mr. Styrax 1
3. Mr. Hecatomb Styrax, the owner of a large estate and of large muscles,
4. A 'blue' and a climber of mountains, has married at the age of 28,
5. He being at that age a virgin,
6. The term Virgo' being made male in mediaeval latinity;
7. His ineptitudes
8. Have driven his wife from one religious excess to another.
9. She has abandoned the vicar
10. For he was lacking in vehemence;
11. She is now the high-priestess
12. Of a modern and ethical cult,
13. And even now Mr. Styrax
14. Does not believe in asthetics.

15. 2
16. His brother has taken to gipsies,
17. But the son-in-law of Mr. H. Styrax
18. Objects to perfumed cigarettes.
19. In the parlance of Niccolo Machiavelli:
20. ‘Thus things proceed in their circle';
21. And thus the empire is maintained.

22. II
23. Clara

24. At sixteen she was a potential celebrity
25. With a distaste for caresses.
26. She now writes to me from a convent;
27. Her life is obscure and troubled;
28. Her second husband will not divorce her;
29. Her mind is, as ever, uncultivated,
30. And no issue presents itself.
31. She does not desire her children,
32. Or any more children.
33. Her ambition is vague and indefinite,
34. She will neither stay in, nor come out.

71. 2
72. And in the home of the novelist
73. There is a satin-like bow on an harp.
74. You enter and pass hall after hall,
75. Conservatory follows conservatory,
76. Lilies lift their white symbolical cups,
77. Whence their symbolical pollen has been excerpted,
78. Near them I noticed an harp
79. And the blue satin ribbon,
80. And the copy of 4Hatha Yoga'
81. And the neat piles of unopened, unopening books,

82. And she spoke to me of the monarch,
83. And of the purity of her soul.
