1. There was Rundle, Station Master,
2. An' Beazeley of the Rail,
3. An' 'Ackman, Commissariat,
4. An' Donkin' o' the Jail;
5. An' Blake, Conductor-Sargent,
6. Our Master twice was 'e,
7. With 'im that kept the Europe-shop,
8. Old Framjee Eduljee.

9. Outside -- "Sergeant!  Sir!  Salute!  Salaam!"
10. Inside -- "Brother", an' it doesn't do no 'arm.
11. We met upon the Level an' we parted on the Square,
12. An' I was Junior Deacon in my Mother-Lodge out there!
13. We'd Bola Nath, Accountant,
14. An' Saul the Aden Jew,
15. An' Din Mohammed, draughtsman
16. Of the Survey Office too;
17. There was Babu Chuckerbutty,
18. An' Amir Singh the Sikh,
19. An' Castro from the fittin'-sheds,
20. The Roman Catholick!

21. We 'adn't good regalia,
22. An' our Lodge was old an' bare,
23. But we knew the Ancient Landmarks,
24. An' we kep' 'em to a hair;
25. An' lookin' on it backwards
26. It often strikes me thus,
27. There ain't such things as infidels,
28. Excep', per'aps, it's us.

29. For monthly, after Labour,
30. We'd all sit down and smoke
31. (We dursn't give no banquits,
32. Lest a Brother's caste were broke),
33. An' man on man got talkin'
34. Religion an' the rest,
35. An' every man comparin'
36. Of the God 'e knew the best.

37. So man on man got talkin',
38. An' not a Brother stirred
39. Till mornin' waked the parrots
40. An' that dam' brain-fever-bird;
41. We'd say 'twas 'ighly curious,
42. An' we'd all ride 'ome to bed,
43. With Mo'ammed, God, an' Shiva
44. Changin' pickets in our 'ead.

45. Full oft on Guv'ment service
46. This rovin' foot 'ath pressed,
47. An' bore fraternal greetin's
48. To the Lodges east an' west,
49. Accordin' as commanded
50. From Kohat to Singapore,
51. But I wish that I might see them
52. In my Mother-Lodge once more!

53. I wish that I might see them,
54. My Brethren black an' brown,
55. With the trichies smellin' pleasant
56. An' the ~hog-darn~ passin' down;                          [Cigar-lighter.]
57. An' the old khansamah snorin'                                     [Butler.]
58. On the bottle-khana floor,                                       [Pantry.]
59. Like a Master in good standing
60. With my Mother-Lodge once more!

61. Outside -- "Sergeant!  Sir!  Salute!  Salaam!"
62. Inside -- "Brother", an' it doesn't do no 'arm.
63. We met upon the Level an' we parted on the Square,
64. An' I was Junior Deacon in my Mother-Lodge out there!
