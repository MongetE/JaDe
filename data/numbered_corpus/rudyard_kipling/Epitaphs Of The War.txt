5. a servant

6. We were together since the War began.
7. He was my servant—and the better man.


8. a son

9. My son was killed while laughing at some jest.    I would I knew
10. What it was, and it might serve me in a time when jests are few.


11. an only son

12. I have slain none except my Mother.    She
13. (Blessing her slayer) died of grief for me.


14. ex-clerk

15. Pity not!    The Army gave
16. Freedom to a timid slave:
17. In which Freedom did he find
18. Strength of body, will, and mind:
19. By which strength he came to prove
20. Mirth, Companionship, and Love:
21. For which Love to Death he went:
22. In which Death he lies content.


23. the wonder

24. Body and Spirit I surrendered whole
25. To harsh Instructors—and received a soul . . .
26. If mortal man could change me through and through
27. From all I was—what may The God not do?


28. hindu sepoy in france

29. This man in his own country prayed we know not to what Powers.
30. We pray Them to reward him for his bravery in ours.


31. the coward

32. I could not look on Death, which being known,
33. Men led me to him, blindfold and alone.


34. shock

35. My name, my speech, my self I had forgot.
36. My wife and children came—I knew them not.
37. I died.    My Mother followed.    At her call
38. And on her bosom I remembered all.


39. a grave near cairo

40. Gods of the Nile, should this stout fellow here
41. Get out—get out!    He knows not shame nor fear.


42. pelicans in the wilderness
43. A Grave near Halfa

44. The blown sand heaps on me, that none may learn
45. Where I am laid for whom my children grieve . . .
46. O wings that beat at dawning, ye return
47. Out of the desert to your young at eve!


48. two canadian memorials

49. i

50. We giving all gained all.
51. Neither lament us nor praise.
52. Only in all things recall,
53. It is Fear, not Death that slays.

54. ii

55. From little towns in a far land we came,
56. To save our honour and a world aflame.
57. By little towns in a far land we sleep;
58. And trust that world we won for you to keep!


59. the favour

60. Death favoured me from the first, well knowing I could not endure
61. To wait on him day by day.    He quitted my betters and came
62. Whistling over the fields, and, when he had made all sure,
63. "Thy line is at end," he said, "but at least I have saved its name."


64. the beginner

65. On the first hour of my first day
66. In the front trench I fell.
67. (Children in boxes at a play
68. Stand up to watch it well.)


69. r.a.f. (aged eighteen)

70. Laughing through clouds, his milk-teeth still unshed,
71. Cities and men he smote from overhead.
72. His deaths delivered, he returned to play
73. Childlike, with childish things now put away.


74. the refined man

75. I was of delicate mind.    I stepped aside for my needs,
76. Disdaining the common office.    I was seen from afar and killed . . .
77. How is this matter for mirth?    Let each man be judged by his deeds.
78. I have paid my price to live with myself on the terms that I willed.


79. native water-carrier (m.e.f.)

80. Prometheus brought down fire to men,
81. This brought up water.
82. The Gods are jealous—now, as then,
83. Giving no quarter.


84. bombed in london

85. On land and sea I strove with anxious care
86. To escape conscription.    It was in the air!


87. the sleepy sentinal

88. Faithless the watch that I kept: now I have none to keep.
89. I was slain because I slept: now I am slain I sleep.
90. Let no man reproach me again, whatever watch is unkept—
91. I sleep because I am slain.    They slew me because I slept.


92. batteries out of ammunition

93. If any mourn us in the workshop, say
94. We died because the shift kept holiday.


95. common form

96. If any question why we died,
97. Tell them, because our fathers lied.


98. a dead statesman

99. I could not dig: I dared not rob:
100. Therefore I lied to please the mob.
101. Now all my lies are proved untrue
102. And I must face the men I slew.
103. What tale shall serve me here among
104. Mine angry and defrauded young?


105. the rebel

106. If I had clamoured at Thy Gate
107. For gift of Life on Earth,
108. And, thrusting through the souls that wait,
109. Flung headlong into birth—
110. Even then, even then, for gin and snare
111. About my pathway spread,
112. Lord, I had mocked Thy thoughtful care
113. Before I joined the Dead!
114. But now? . . . I was beneath Thy Hand
115. Ere yet the Planets came.
116. And now—though Planets pass, I stand
117. The witness to Thy shame!


118. the obedient

119. Daily, though no ears attended,
120. Did my prayers arise.
121. Daily, though no fire descended,
122. Did I sacrifice.
123. Though my darkness did not lift,
124. Though I faced no lighter odds,
125. Though the Gods bestowed no gift,
126. None the less,
127. None the less, I served the Gods!


128. a drifter off tarentum

129. He from the wind-bitten North with ship and companions descended,
130. Searching for eggs of death spawned by invisible hulls.
131. Many he found and drew forth.    Of a sudden the fishery ended
132. In flame and a clamours breath known to the eye-pecking gulls.


133. destroyer in collision

134. For Fog and Fate no charm is found
135. To lighten or amend.
136. I, hurrying to my bride, was drowned—
137. Cut down by my best friend.


138. convoy escort

139. I was a shepherd to fools
140. Causelessly bold or afraid.
141. They would not abide by my rules.
142. Yet they escaped.    For I stayed.


143. unknown female corpse

144. Headless, lacking foot and hand,
145. Horrible I come to land.
146. I beseech all women's sons
147. Know I was a mother once.


148. raped and revenged

149. One used and butchered me: another spied
150. Me broken—for which thing an hundred died.
151. So it was learned among the heathen hosts
152. How much a freeborn woman's favour costs.


153. salonikan grave

154. I have watched a thousand days
155. Push out and crawl into night
156. Slowly as tortoises.
157. Now I, too, follow these.
158. It is fever, and not the fight—
159. Time, not battle,—that slays.


160. the bridegroom

161. Call me not false, beloved,
162. If, from thy scarce-known breast
163. So little time removed,
164. In other arms I rest.

165. For this more ancient bride,
166. Whom coldly I embrace,
167. Was constant at my side
168. Before I saw thy face.

169. Our marriage, often set—
170. By miracle delayed—
171. At last is consummate,
172. And cannot be unmade.

173. Live, then, whom Life shall cure,
174. Almost, of Memory,
175. And leave us to endure
176. Its immortality.


177. v.a.d. (mediterranean)

178. Ah, would swift ships had never been, for then we ne'er had found,
179. These harsh Aegean rocks between, this little virgin drowned,
180. Whom neither spouse nor child shall mourn, but men she nursed through pain
181. And—certain keels for whose return the heathen look in vain.
