1. "WHA is that at my bower-door?"
2. "O wha is it but Findlay!"
3. "Then gae your gate, ye'se nae be here:"
4. "Indeed maun I," quo' Findlay;
5. "What mak' ye, sae like a thief?"
6. "O come and see," quo' Findlay;
7. "Before the morn ye'll work mischief:"
8. "Indeed will I," quo' Findlay.

9. "Gif I rise and let you in"—
10. "Let me in," quo' Findlay;
11. "Ye'll keep me waukin wi' your din;"
12. "Indeed will I," quo' Findlay;
13. "In my bower if ye should stay"—
14. "Let me stay," quo' Findlay;
15. "I fear ye'll bide till break o' day;"
16. "Indeed will I," quo' Findlay.

17. "Here this night if ye remain"—
18. "I'll remain," quo' Findlay;
19. "I dread ye'll learn the gate again;"
20. "Indeed will I," quo' Findlay."What may pass within this bower"—
21. "Let it pass," quo' Findlay;
22. "Ye maun conceal till your last hour:"
23. "Indeed will I," quo' Findlay.
