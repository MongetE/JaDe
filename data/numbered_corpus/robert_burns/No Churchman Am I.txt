1. NO churchman am I for to rail and to write,
2. No statesman nor soldier to plot or to fight,
3. No sly man of business contriving a snare,
4. For a big-belly'd bottle's the whole of my care.


5. The peer I don't envy, I give him his bow;
6. I scorn not the peasant, though ever so low;
7. But a club of good fellows, like those that are here,
8. And a bottle like this, are my glory and care.


9. Here passes the squire on his brother-his horse;
10. There centum per centum, the cit with his purse;
11. But see you the Crown how it waves in the air?
12. There a big-belly'd bottle still eases my care.


13. The wife of my bosom, alas! she did die;
14. for sweet consolation to church I did fly;
15. I found that old Solomon proved it fair,
16. That a big-belly'd bottle's a cure for all care.


17. I once was persuaded a venture to make;
18. A letter inform'd me that all was to wreck;
19. But the pursy old landlord just waddl'd upstairs,
20. With a glorious bottle that ended my cares.
