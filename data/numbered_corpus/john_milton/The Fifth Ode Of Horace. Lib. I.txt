1. Quis multa gracilis te puer in Rosa
2. Rendred almost word for word without Rhyme according to the
3. Latin Measure, as near as the Language permit.

4. WHAT slender Youth bedew'd with liquid odours
5. Courts thee on Roses in some pleasant Cave,
6. Pyrrha for whom bind'st thou
7. In wreaths thy golden Hair,
8. Plain in thy neatness; O how oft shall he
9. On Faith and changed Gods complain: and Seas
10. Rough with black winds and storms
11. Unwonted shall admire:
12. Who now enjoyes thee credulous, all Gold,
13. Who alwayes vacant, alwayes amiable
14. Hopes thee; of flattering gales
15. Unmindfull.  Hapless they
16. To whom thou untry'd seem'st fair.  Me in my vow'd
17. Picture the sacred wall declares t' have hung
18. My dank and dropping weeds
19. To the stern God of Sea.

20. [The Latin text follows.]
