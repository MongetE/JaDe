1. XIII

2. To Mr. H. Lawes, on his Aires.

3. Harry whose tuneful and well measur'd Song
4. First taught our English Musick how to span
5. Words with just note and accent, not to scan
6. With Midas Ears, committing short and long;
7. Thy worth and skill exempts thee from the throng,
8. With praise enough for Envy to look wan;
9. To after age thou shalt be writ the man,
10. That with smooth aire couldst humor best our tongue
11. Thou honour'st Verse, and Verse must send her wing
12. To honour thee, the Priest of Phoebus Quire
13. That tun'st their happiest lines in Hymn or Story
14. Dante shall give Fame leave to set thee higher
15. Then his Casella, whom he woo'd to sing
16. Met in the milder shades of Purgatory.
