1. XII. On the same.

2. I did but prompt the age to quit their cloggs
3. By the known rules of antient libertie,
4. When strait a barbarous noise environs me
5. Of Owles and Cuckoes, Asses, Apes and Doggs.
6. As when those Hinds that were transform'd to Froggs
7. Raild at Latona's twin-born progenie
8. Which after held the Sun and Moon in fee.
9. But this is got by casting Pearl to Hoggs;
10. That bawle for freedom in their senceless mood,
11. And still revolt when truth would set them free.
12. Licence they mean when they cry libertie;
13. For who loves that, must first be wise and good;
14. But from that mark how far they roave we see
15. For all this wast of wealth, and loss of blood.
